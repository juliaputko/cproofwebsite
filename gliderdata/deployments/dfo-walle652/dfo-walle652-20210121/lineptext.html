Line P is a monitoring transect in the Northeast Pacific spanning from the southern tip of Vancouver Island in Canada towards Station Papa, approximately 1500 km offshore to the west. It is one of the longest running monitoring transect in the world with accessible data starting from 1957. The origins of Line P can be traced back to April 1959 when seven hydrographic casts were started along the line leading towards Station Papa. Since then, the sampling extended to more stations along the line. Currently, Line P contains 27 stations along with 3 yearly cruises along the transect which regularly samples the physical properties such as temperature and salinity, as well as ecological properties such as dissolved oxygen and nutrients.
